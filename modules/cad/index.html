Background

The University of Bath support two CAD packages: AutoDesk Fusion 360 and AutoDesk Inventor Professional. Both are free for educational use and can be downloaded by the students on their own devices. The software can be downloaded here.

One of the great things with using AutoDesk CAD is the wealth of analytical & manufacturing packages that your models can then be imported into where you can further evaluate and manufacture your designs. You will be using these packages in your other courses such as Finite Element Analysis, Computer Numerical Control and Computational Fluid Dynamics.

Fusion 360

Description to go here...

Advantages

  • System Agnostic - Can be used on Windows, Mac & Linux (through the web browser)
  • Android and iOS - Can view and comment designs wherever you are
  • Product Data Management built-in (Cloud-based storage and version control)
  • Collaborative Projects - Share and work on the same projects as your friends
  • Offline and online working modes
  • Continually being updated with new and improved functionality
  • Full Computer Aided Engineering (CAE) tool-chain including animation, rendering, Finite Element Analysis, Computer Numerical Control & 3D Printing

Disadvantages

  • Still lacking some drawing features
  • Works best when connected to the internet

Inventor Professional

Description to go here...

Advantages

  • Classical CAD package that is very mature in its development and has more complex features
  • Can be used with AutoDesk Vault for Product Data Management

Disadvantages

  • Manual storage and management of parts & assemblies unless Autodesk Vault is used
  • Difficult to collaborate on projects if manual storage and management of files used
  • Windows only compatibility
